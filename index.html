Jean Pinsolle

PhD in Statistical Signal Processing and Machine Learning

I am interested in statistical signal processing, inverse problems, optimization and modern generative models for signal and image processing.

My research focuses on the adaptation and analysis of probabilistic and machine learning methods for signal processing applications, with a particular interest in diffusion models, flow matching approaches and uncertainty quantification.

During my PhD, I worked on radar pulse deinterleaving problems, studying estimation methods for mixtures of stochastic processes together with discrete optimization heuristics for practical reconstruction algorithms.

More broadly, I am interested in the connections between statistical inference, stochastic modeling, inverse problems and learning-based approaches for signal processing.

Research Interests

Signal Processing Inverse Problems Diffusion Models Flow Matching Statistical Learning Optimization Bayesian Methods Uncertainty Quantification

My current interests include generative models for inverse problems, stochastic surrogate modeling, uncertainty quantification and statistical estimation for structured signals.

I am particularly interested in understanding the theoretical properties of modern generative approaches and their connections with classical signal processing and Bayesian inference methods.

Teaching

I am interested in teaching signal processing, applied mathematics, probability, statistics and machine learning.

I would particularly like to contribute to courses combining theoretical foundations and practical applications, ranging from classical signal processing methods to modern statistical learning approaches.

In the longer term, I would also like to develop specialized courses related to current research topics in signal processing and generative modeling.
